Latest Cabinet Refinishing Trends for Roseville Kitchens

Kitchen cabinets are the focal point of any kitchen, setting the tone for the entire space. As we approach 2025, cabinet refinishing continues to be one of the most popular and cost-effective ways for Roseville homeowners to transform their kitchens without the expense and disruption of a full renovation.

At Precision Finish, we've refinished hundreds of kitchen cabinets throughout Roseville and have our finger on the pulse of emerging trends. In this guide, we'll explore the latest cabinet refinishing trends that are shaping Roseville kitchens and how you can incorporate them into your home.

Color Trends in Cabinet Refinishing

Color choices for kitchen cabinets continue to evolve, with several distinct trends emerging in Roseville homes:

Warm Whites and Creams

While bright white cabinets dominated the 2010s, we're now seeing a shift toward softer, warmer whites and creams that create a more inviting atmosphere while maintaining a clean, fresh look.

Popular Shades:

Alabaster
Swiss Coffee
Creamy
White Dove

Rich Blues and Greens

Bold, saturated colors are making a strong statement in Roseville kitchens, with deep blues and greens leading the trend. These colors add personality and depth while still feeling sophisticated and timeless.

Popular Shades:

Naval
Hague Blue
Hunter Green
Pewter Green

Designer Insight

"We're seeing many Roseville homeowners opt for a mix of painted and wood-finished cabinets in the same kitchen. This might take the form of a wood island with painted perimeter cabinets, or wood upper cabinets with painted lowers. This approach adds visual interest and depth to the kitchen while maintaining a cohesive look." - Nic Guedenet, Precision Finish

Simplified Shaker

Shaker-style cabinets continue to dominate in Roseville kitchens, but with a more refined, streamlined approach. The traditional five-piece Shaker door is evolving toward slimmer rails and stiles for a more contemporary look.

Trending Details:

Narrower frame profiles: Creating a more delicate, refined appearance

Minimal or no beveling: Offering cleaner lines and easier cleaning

Subtle details: Small beading or edge profiles that add interest without overwhelming
